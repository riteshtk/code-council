Executive Summary

FastAPI is a mature, well-structured framework with strong community adoption. However, the council identified 2 critical findings related to bus factor concentration and an unpatched CVE in Starlette. The dependency injection subsystem shows signs of architectural strain — high churn, repeated rewrites, and growing complexity. Of 5 proposals, 4 passed and 1 deadlocked on the question of sync middleware replacement. The council recommends immediate action on the CVE patch and test coverage gaps, with a phased approach to the DI refactor.

Critical Findings

CRITICAL Archaeologist
Bus factor of 1 in 12 core modules. 78% of commits to routing/, dependencies/, and security/ come from a single contributor. No co-maintainers have write history in these areas.
Implication: A single departure would leave critical subsystems without institutional knowledge.
CRITICAL Skeptic
CVE-2024-XXXX in Starlette 0.36.x. The pinned Starlette version has a known path traversal vulnerability. The fix is available in 0.37.2+.
Implication: Any deployment serving static files is exposed. Upgrade is non-breaking.
HIGH Archaeologist
43 unresolved TODOs aged 2+ years. The oldest dates to 2021. 12 reference features that were never implemented. 8 mark known bugs with no linked issues.
Implication: Accumulated technical promises erode developer trust and make triage harder.

Proposals & Votes

P-3-v2: Extract DI Bounded Context (Facade-First)
Effort: L
Extract the dependency injection system into a self-contained bounded context, starting with a facade layer that wraps existing internals. Consumers migrate to the facade incrementally over 2-3 releases. A lint rule blocks new direct imports to dependencies/ internals.
